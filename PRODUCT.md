# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A single user (the owner) — a single user who runs their monthly finances on a
monthly 23rd-to-22nd cycle. glance-first is a hard design constraint: the answer to "how am I
doing this month?" must be reachable in one glance, one scroll, no nested menus. The
dashboard is opened on a laptop, occasionally a phone, usually once or twice a month
after statements are processed, and briefly each week to check pace.

## Product Purpose

A self-contained local HTML dashboard (`output/dashboard.html`) generated by a Python
pipeline. It turns three Bahrain bank/card statements + a plan spreadsheet into: headline
income/spend/net, plan-vs-actual with RAG status, category breakdown, standing leak flags,
subscription changes, sinking-fund progress, a forward cash-flow forecast, and a review
queue. Success = the user understands their month and knows the one action to take
(the investable surplus / suggested transfer) without reading anything twice.

## Brand Personality

Blunt, precise, calm. Three words: **honest, legible, unshowy.** It reads like a good
terminal report or a well-set financial statement, not a consumer budgeting app. It never
oversells a number or hides a bad one; a deficit is shown in red and named, not softened.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards with cream/sand backgrounds, pill-heavy cards, and gradient hero
  metrics. This is not a Mint/YNAB clone.
- The "hero-metric template" (one giant gradient number with small supporting stats).
- Cluttered budgeting apps with dozens of donut charts and emoji noise.
- Anything that looks AI-generated: identical card grids, tiny tracked uppercase eyebrows on
  every section, glassmorphism, over-rounded 32px cards.

## Design Principles

1. **Findings first.** The most decision-relevant number (net, surplus, the biggest leak)
   is visible before any detail. Order by what drives action, not by data model.
2. **Honest numbers.** Deficits red and named; extrapolations labelled; "uncategorised %"
   shown, never hidden. Never dress up a bad month.
3. **One-glance legibility.** High contrast, real hierarchy through scale/weight, monospace
   for figures so columns align and scan. glance-first: flat, no chrome for chrome's sake.
4. **Serves the data.** Visual choices exist to make the numbers faster to read (aligned
   columns, RAG colour, sparklines), never to decorate.
5. **Theme-aware and self-contained.** One file, no external assets, respects light/dark.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA for all text (body ≥ 4.5:1, large ≥ 3:1) in both light and dark themes. RAG status
must not rely on colour alone (pair with the emoji/glyph and the sign of the number).
Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Numeric-first so the dashboard is usable without colour.
